# G. Change, Adoption & Communications

**Source:** SRC-001, SRC-002 (Day 1); SRC-008 (D2S1), SRC-009 (D2S2) — Day 2 re-baseline.

## Impacted users
- Customers across migrated country/business sites and secure-web journeys (mix of old/new designs during transition).
- Internal teams: publishers, designers, BAs, regional teams, service team, call centre.
- Business units submitting design requests (currently unstructured except OMF).

## Behaviour / ways-of-working changes
- Move to structured design briefs using platform components (OMF is the model; others must adopt).
- Adopt entry/exit gates and handover criteria between teams.
- Adopt single shared knowledge system (currently missing → delays).
- Adopt OMDS governance (component lifecycle, approval SLAs, exception process).

## Training / documentation needs
- Onboarding for new team members needs clear, actionable deliverables (not strategic input).
- Documentation of journeys via journey maps so journeys aren't lost in migration.
- Documented & approved design/template approach.
- Content standards documented inside the design system.

## Adoption risks / resistance points
- Business units resisting standardised templates → mitigated by offering template variants (buy-in).
- Non-migrated sites drifting from OMDS without controlled adoption model (RSK-012).
- Cluster/SME engagement gaps blocking content (RSK-009/016).
- Capacity strain reducing quality if not managed (RSK-002).

## Communications required
- Call-centre + internal communications for go-live (ISS-005).
- E-commerce comms before EasiPlus changes (marketing spend impact).
- **Notify e-commerce (Nthabi) of URL changes** — ~95% of URLs changing affects GA tracking + exec sales/traffic reporting (ACT-046, RSK-021).
- **SEO engagement-frequency requirements communicated to the CFO** (ACT-012).
- **Faoli Bank** distinct migration/branding requirements communicated to relevant teams (DEC-013).
- Change-expectation comms for non-migrated sites (what changes now / stays as-is / moves later).
- Rollout/demo approach to showcase target experience, validate enhanced components, manage expectations.

## Knowledge & documentation (S2/S3)
- Stand up a **centralised documentation/knowledge system** (processes, history, relationships) — root-cause fix for delays (ACT-054, ISS-008, RSK-020).
- Hold an alignment session on design-system taxonomy (compositions/components/patterns/templates + naming) (ACT-048).

## Business readiness / operational handover
- Go-live readiness checklist (PGW testing, QAG, ServiceNow, security, comms, decommission) — ACT-013.
- Operational handover incl. support model for migrated sites.
- Maintain existing design estate during transition to avoid inconsistency.

# Day 2 re-baseline / update (SRC-008 D2S1, SRC-009 D2S2)

> Day 2 added concrete change levers: **onboarding new members/tenants to standards**, **sponsor expectation-management** (not all year-end goals delivered), **weekly playback/visibility**, and a structured **participant-feedback** loop. The Day 1 adoption content above remains; the items below add the Day 2 baseline.

## G1. Onboarding new members / tenants to standards (DEC-019, ACT-067)

- Define an **onboarding process and deliverables** for new team members/tenants joining in June (ACT-067).
- Because only **Gareth & Natalie** build templates and the team **gatekeeps** publishing (DEC-019), any new team/tenant must be **trained to the established standards before they can publish**.
- Formalise a **way of working** for onboarding new tenants/teams so processes and standards stay consistent (mitigates governance/template drift RSK-027 and SPOF RSK-026).
- Country teams onboard into the **same** standards/process: central provides design/IA/nav/content guidelines; country teams audit, map to audience groupings, rewrite/create content (DEC-025). Onboarding/support for **external teams with limited resources** is still **undecided** (RSK-032) — flagged.

## G2. Sponsor expectation-management (RSK-031) — explicit

- Day 2 explicitly acknowledged that **not all year-end goals will be delivered**; the team will have the **evidence and support** needed to communicate **what can be achieved and the value it brings**.
- Frame as **phased value** (Personal first, then Corporate/Business/Wealth — DEC-015) and manage sponsor expectations against the linear-delivery gap (RSK-024). The plan is expected to **change multiple times before year-end**; alignment is on goal/approach, not a fixed line.
- `Flagged for executive escalation` — sponsor/steering communication required.

## G3. Weekly playback / visibility (DEC-027, ACT-082)

- **Weekly playback** sessions to review template progress and give the **technical team early visibility** (ACT-082), plus the **twice-weekly 30-min** leadership check-ins (ACT-062).
- Share **pictures/video** of the physical board/roadmap into team chat so **remote/virtual participants** can follow the roadmap playback.
- Unified Jira construct gives all teams a shared, visible tracking view (DEC-027).

## G4. Participant feedback (ACT-084)

- Collect **written feedback** from all participants on the two days + **virtual feedback** from online participants via chat, including ideas to make sessions **repeatable** (ACT-084).
- Feedback themes raised: strong value in **face-to-face / in-person collaboration**, "having the right people in the room", being **heard and understood**, and avoiding repeating past mistakes (Siva) — inputs to improving future sessions.
- **Open:** optimal **cadence/format** for ongoing planning sessions still undecided (QST-026).

## G5. Day 2 communications additions
- **Country-team workshop(s):** document the migration + content-audit process and run alignment workshops with country teams (ACT-083, DEC-025).
- **Tenant operating-model comms:** communicate future funding/ownership partnership expectations to tenants (DEC-026).
- Continued change-expectation comms for non-migrated/smaller sites (quick nav+footer retrofit — PRK-013).

## G6. Adoption risks (Day 2)
- New tenants/teams bypassing standards → governance/template drift (RSK-027).
- Onboarding/support gap for resource-constrained country teams (RSK-032).
- Expectation gap if sponsors expect full year-end delivery (RSK-031).
