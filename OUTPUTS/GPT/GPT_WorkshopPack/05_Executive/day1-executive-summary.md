## 1. Day 1 executive summary
Day 1 (across Sessions 1–3) aligned the workshop teams on the programme's goals, surfaced the full delivery landscape, ran a per-workstream whiteboard exercise, and closed with a frank scope-vs-timeline reality check. The defining outcome is a clear recognition that the work labelled a "migration" in fact carries substantial **transformation** scope (OMDS adoption, V2 templates, IA changes, analytics/observability, automated testing, revised publishing workflow, decommissioning). The closing session made the gap explicit: the **full discussed scope implies ~end-2027 delivery**, while the budget fixes the deadline at **end-November (~5-6 months)** — forcing a **tactical vs strategic** choice (e.g. a possible navigation-only first cut). This ambiguity, plus material capacity constraints (notably a near single-person publishing function) and the absence of a centralised knowledge system, frames everything. Overall programme position: **Amber / At Risk**.

## 2. Key themes
- **Scope ambiguity (migration vs transformation)** is the master issue — it changes scope, effort, budget, timeline and risk.
- **Capacity is the binding constraint** — publishing (2 publishers, no dedicated OMAR resource), copywriting, design, front-end (post-PI2 exit) and BA.
- **OMDS is a governance layer**, not just a UI library — needs an operating model and design authority.
- **Readiness gates are immature** — SEO not ready, content readiness, environments, URL audit, go-live checklist.
- **Secure web & regional servicing** are concrete delivery risks (Namibia, Botswana 1/9).
- **Foundations before scale** — IA, navigation, templates, content standards, component audit must precede broad rollout.
