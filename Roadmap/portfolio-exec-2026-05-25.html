How to read these numbers — pages vs delivery actions

📄 Pages
URLs that have to land on the new platform, redirect, consolidate, or be deleted. The QA readiness bar tracks how many pages are through nav / footer / copy / sign-off / in-prod checks.
🛠 Delivery actions
Tickets in Jira (GWPM / GWID / GWSM). Mostly component builds, bug fixes, content tasks — not 1:1 with pages. One ticket can serve hundreds of pages, or none. The % done line tracks workload completion.
⚠ Why they don't reconcile
Pages count assets; actions count work units. An estate can be 0% QA and 80% delivery (build done, QA hasn't started) or 100% live with active tickets (post-launch defects).
High QA · High delivery
Truly done. Pages live, tickets resolved.
e.g. Malawi (100% / 100%), Bula Tsela.
Low QA · High delivery
Platform ready, QA pending. Foundations built, pages just haven't been pushed through QA yet.
e.g. Eswatini (0% / 82%).
High QA · Low delivery
Investigate. Pages passing checks but tickets still open — possible scope or bug debt.
Not currently observed.
Low QA · Low delivery
Genuinely not started. No QA progress and no tickets logged.
e.g. OMSA brand suite, most of OMAR Africa.

Caveat for steerco: a 0% delivery line means no tickets reference this estate yet — that could be "not started" or "not yet ticketed". Until work is logged, it doesn't show. Treat low-delivery estates as a planning gap, not just a delivery gap.

Top delivery risks

If any of these go uncontrolled, 31-Dec slips. Each has a concrete mitigation owned by an existing team.

SA cutover slips → Wave 2 unravels
Critical
SA's Q4 cutover assumes nav + footer + 37 components land in Wave 1. Any slip there cascades through Wave 2/3 and the Dec deadline.
Mitigation: Lock Wave 1 scope in May steerco. Treat any Wave 1 blocker as P1. Pre-stage SA hypercare so cutover doesn't compete for the same team.
11 OMAR Africa estates in 2 months
Critical
Wave 3 needs ≥4 estates cutover per month — far beyond the current 1-estate-per-quarter cadence (Malawi Jan, Bula Tsela Mar).
Mitigation: Country leads run in parallel; centralise QA & ops; use SA/Eswatini playbook; restrict per-country scope to 'as-is + critical content'.
OMSA brand suite untracked
High
OMAI / OMUT / Institutional / OMCOM have page lists but no QA tracker, no Jira tickets logged. We can't size the work.
Mitigation: First Wave 1 deliverable for each: open a tracker, log tickets, run the 'as-is migratable' triage SA already has.
Capacity collision: Wave 2 + Wave 3 overlap
High
Oct/Nov sees SA cutover, Secure SA cutover, brand suite cutover, and OMAR rollouts simultaneously. Same QA, ops, and comms teams.
Mitigation: Stagger cutover nights; pre-build per-estate runbooks; book ops capacity in May; consider freeze on non-critical change after 1 Oct.
AI scope undecided
High
If steerco confirms AI in-scope after May, every estate's plan window must be re-examined for AI dependencies.
Mitigation: Force the decision in May steerco. Default-out is the safer landing for 31-Dec.
Country legal / brand sign-off delay
High
Each OMAR market and OMSA brand has its own legal/brand approval flow. Historically 2–4 week delays per estate.
Mitigation: Engage country leads in May; pre-circulate templated content for legal review during Wave 1; surface blocked sign-offs at every steerco.
